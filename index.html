Abstract

Text-to-3D aims to synthesize high-quality 3D assets from natural language prompts; however, single-stage pipelines based on 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) still suffer from cross-view appearance and geometric inconsistencies and lack a unified post-optimization mechanism. We propose Anchor-Guided Progressive Optimization (AGPO), a lightweight, training-free framework designed to improve cross-view consistency in text-to-3D generation. Within AGPO, Anchor-Guided Progressive Propagation (AGPP) leverages geometric relationships between adjacent views to progressively propagate reliable structural and semantic constraints across the view space, yielding globally consistent 3D reconstructions. Through occlusion-aware reprojection and multi-view correspondence constraints, AGPP enforces coherent geometry and appearance between anchor views and their neighboring views. Furthermore, an Uncertainty-Aware Reweighting (UAR) mechanism adaptively adjusts pixel-level supervision based on depth consistency, effectively suppressing artifacts in occluded or uncertain regions. Compatible with mainstream 3DGS-based text-to-3D pipelines, AGPO improves Contrastive Language--Image Pre-training (CLIP) similarity by 5.76% and reduces the reprojection mean squared error (MSE) by 22.61%. Compared with representative text-to-3D baselines, AGPO maintains stable geometric and appearance consistency without requiring additional training or architectural modifications.
