Abstract

Text-to-3D aims to synthesize high-quality 3D assets from natural language prompts, yet single-stage pipelines based on 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) still suffer from cross-view appearance and geometric inconsistencies, lacking a unified post-optimization mechanism. We propose AGPO (Anchor-Guided Progressive Optimization), a lightweight and training-free framework for refining existing 3DGS models. AGPO leverages geometric relationships between adjacent views to progressively expand local optimization results into globally consistent reconstructions. Through occlusion-aware reprojection and multi-view correspondence constraints, the method propagates structural and semantic consistency from anchor views to their neighboring views, achieving unified geometry and appearance. Furthermore, an Uncertainty-Aware Reweighting (UAR) mechanism adaptively adjusts pixel-level supervision based on depth consistency, effectively suppressing artifacts in occluded or uncertain regions. On LGM-initialized models, AGPO improves CLIP similarity by 6.65%, reduces multi-view LPIPS by 3.49%, and decreases reprojection MSE by 23.24%. Compared with DreamGaussian and GaussianDreamer, AGPO maintains stable geometric and appearance consistency without extra training or architectural changes. These results demonstrate that anchor-guided progressive optimization with uncertainty-aware regularization provides an effective and practical solution for enhancing multi-view consistency in Text-to-3D.
